RAA

Recursive Agent Architecture

A domain-agnostic framework for stress-testing analytical conclusions through adversarial validation. RAA deploys four reasoning agents that challenge claims from distinct perspectives, reducing confirmation bias and strengthening strategic reasoning.

Overview

Adversarial Validation

RAA addresses a fundamental limitation in strategic analysis: confirmation bias and analytical blindspots. By deploying four adversarial reasoning agents that recursively challenge conclusions, the framework stress-tests claims before they inform high-stakes decisions.

Each agent operates from a distinct analytical perspective, ensuring comprehensive examination of assumptions, evidence quality, logical consistency, and alternative interpretations. The recursive structure means each challenge generates counter-challenges, forcing continuous refinement until analytical consensus or productive disagreement emerges.

Applications

Strategic Assessment: Validating intelligence conclusions, competitive analysis, scenario planning.

Investment Decisions: Stress-testing investment theses, due diligence validation, risk assessment.

Policy Analysis: Challenging policy recommendations, identifying unexamined assumptions, evaluating evidence quality.

Research Validation: Pre-publication review, methodological rigor assessment, alternative interpretation generation.

Future Integration

RAA will be integrated into MIAF to provide per-register validation of innovation assessments. When a user scores a case dimension, RAA agents will challenge that score from multiple analytical perspectives, forcing justification and refinement. This integration transforms MIAF from a framework into a dialectical analytical instrument.
