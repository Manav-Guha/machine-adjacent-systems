MIAF

Military Innovation Assessment Framework

A validated framework for distinguishing genuine innovation from technical modernization. MIAF assesses developments across four dimensions—Cognitive, Irreversibility, Disruption, and Generative—mapped against organizational domains: People, Process, Technology, and Organization.

Framework Overview

The Four Registers

Cognitive: Does the innovation restructure how practitioners conceptualize problems, solutions, or strategic possibilities? Distinguishes between incremental optimization and fundamental reorientation of thought.

Irreversibility: To what degree does adoption create path dependencies that prevent reversion to previous approaches? Measures structural lock-in across technical, organizational, and doctrinal dimensions.

Disruption: How significantly does the innovation alter competitive dynamics, strategic calculations, or operational paradigms? Assesses magnitude of perturbation to existing equilibria.

Generative: What new strategic possibilities, operational concepts, or problem domains emerge from adoption? Evaluates capacity to create previously impossible or unconsidered pathways.

PPTO Mapping

Innovation effects manifest across organizational domains. MIAF's Register × PPTO signature matrix reveals where transformative impact concentrates:

People: Skills, training, roles, human capital requirements

Process: Workflows, doctrine, procedural frameworks

Technology: Hardware, software, material infrastructure

Organization: Structure, command hierarchies, institutional design

Validation

MIAF has been validated against 28+ historical military innovations spanning 150 years, from the stirrup and gunpowder to GPS-guided munitions and network-centric warfare. The framework demonstrates consistent analytical discrimination between genuine innovations (composite scores ≥12) and modernizations (scores <12).
